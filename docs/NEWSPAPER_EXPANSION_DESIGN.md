## INTERNATIONAL PERSPECTIVE: 168 Countries, 168 Media Landscapes

Your game has 168 countries with MediaFreedom, Corruption, IntelligenceLevel, and other stats. **Newspapers should feel radically different depending on where you are.**

### THE CORE IDEA: Media Freedom Changes Everything

| MediaFreedom | What Papers Look Like | Examples |
|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| **90-100** (Very Free) | Investigative journalism, government criticism, whistleblower stories | Norway, Sweden, Finland |
| **70-89** (Mostly Free) | Some self-censorship, corporate influence, but mostly honest | Germany, UK, Japan |
| **50-69** (Partly Free) | State influence visible, some topics taboo, tabloid-heavy | Mexico, India, Brazil |
| **30-49** (Limited) | Heavy censorship, approved narratives, coded dissent | Russia, Turkey, Hungary |
| **0-29** (Not Free) | Pure propaganda, no criticism, state mouthpiece | North Korea, China, Eritrea |

---

### HOW EACH FEATURE CHANGES BY COUNTRY

#### 1. NEWS SOURCES AVAILABLE

**High MediaFreedom (70+)**
- Multiple competing newspapers with different biases
- Independent investigative outlets
- Foreign press accessible
- Underground/alternative press optional

**Low MediaFreedom (30-)**
- State media dominates
- 1-2 "approved" newspapers
- Foreign press blocked or risky to access
- Underground press = only real news (but dangerous)

**Gameplay:** In China, reading foreign news requires VPN (costs money, risks detection). In Sweden, you have 8 sources with diverse perspectives.

---

#### 2. WANTED POSTERS / BOUNTIES

**High MediaFreedom Countries**
- Official police wanted notices
- Bounties posted by victims/families
- Private security company postings
- Clear legal framework

**Low MediaFreedom Countries**
- "Enemy of the State" propaganda posters
- Extrajudicial bounties from government
- Secret police seeking "persons of interest"
- Your face on news = immediate danger

**Gameplay:** Being wanted in Norway means legal trouble. Being wanted in North Korea means death squads.

---

#### 3. JOB BOARDS / CLASSIFIEDS

**High MediaFreedom**
- Legitimate security consulting jobs
- Above-board mercenary work
- Legal job postings with contracts

**Low MediaFreedom**
- Coded messages for underground work
- Government front companies posting
- "Volunteer patriotic duty" = forced conscription risk
- More black market, less legitimate work

**Gameplay:** In Russia, responding to certain ads might be FSB honeypots. In Japan, job postings are straightforward.

---

#### 4. PROPAGANDA MECHANICS

**High MediaFreedom**
- Propaganda exists but is subtle
- Corporate spin, not state control
- Players can counter with truth
- Media outlets will investigate

**Low MediaFreedom**
- Propaganda is MANDATORY reading
- State narratives cannot be contradicted
- Players must pretend to believe
- Counter-propaganda = death sentence

**Gameplay:** In North Korea, you MUST read propaganda daily or be flagged. In USA, propaganda is optional noise.

---

#### 5. INVESTIGATION TRIGGERS

**High MediaFreedom**
- Newspapers actively investigate
- Journalists as potential allies
- Whistleblowers protected
- Investigations lead to legal action

**Low MediaFreedom**
- State controls what gets investigated
- Journalists are government employees
- Whistleblowers disappear
- Investigations only happen against state enemies

**Gameplay:** In Sweden, a newspaper investigation might help you. In China, any investigation is against dissidents (possibly you).

---

#### 6. LETTERS TO EDITOR / PUBLIC VOICE

**High MediaFreedom**
- Real public sentiment visible
- Range of opinions printed
- Criticism of heroes/villains allowed
- Democratic discourse

**Low MediaFreedom**
- All letters are curated/fake
- Only praise for government printed
- Criticism of regime = never published
- "Grassroots support" is manufactured

**Gameplay:** In Germany, letters reflect actual public opinion. In Russia, letters are state-approved sentiment.

### NEW MECHANIC: MEDIA FREEDOM AFFECTS FAME

Your "fame" should work differently based on location:

**High MediaFreedom:**
- Fame = actual recognition
- Can build brand
- Merchandise, sponsorships
- Public loves/hates you based on actions

**Low MediaFreedom:**
- "Fame" = government's narrative
- They decide if you're hero or villain
- No personal brand possible
- State assigns your reputation

**Gameplay:** You might be beloved hero in USA and designated terrorist in China - from same actions.
